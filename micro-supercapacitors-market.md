<p><p>The micro supercapacitor market features three primary types based on their electrode configurations. Thin film electrodes made from nanostructured materials enhance energy density and charge speed, making them ideal for compact applications. In-plane interdigital electrodes offer improved surface area and efficiency, lending themselves to versatile designs in flexible electronics. Three-dimensional (3D) electrodes provide additional capacitance and energy storage capability by maximizing available surface area, catering to applications that require high performance and miniaturization in energy storage solutions.</p></p>
