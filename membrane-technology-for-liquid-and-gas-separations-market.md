<p><p>Membrane technology for liquid and gas separations encompasses various processes that utilize selectively permeable membranes to separate components. Reverse osmosis effectively removes dissolved solids from liquids under pressure. Nanofiltration targets smaller molecules and divalent ions, while ultrafiltration separates larger particles and microorganisms. Microfiltration focuses on removing suspended solids and bacteria. Electrochemical processes involve ion-selective membranes for electrical separations. Each method offers distinct advantages for applications in water treatment, food processing, pharmaceuticals, and industrial gases, optimizing efficiency and sustainability.</p></p>
