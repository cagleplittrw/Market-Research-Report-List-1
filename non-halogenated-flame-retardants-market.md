<p><p>The non-halogenated flame retardants market primarily includes two key types: aluminum hydroxide and phosphorus-based compounds. Aluminum hydroxide acts as a flame barrier by releasing water vapor, effectively cooling the material when exposed to heat. Phosphorus-based flame retardants function by promoting char formation, which insulates the material and slows down combustion. Both types are gaining traction due to increasing regulatory pressure against halogenated substances and a growing emphasis on environmentally friendly and safer alternatives in various industries, including construction and electronics.</p></p>
